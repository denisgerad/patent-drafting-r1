What was changed (4 files)
workflows/scrutiny_workflow.py — New 2-pass RAG strategy. Pass 1 uses a broad neutral query ("field of invention abstract summary relates to") specifically designed to retrieve the abstract/field section. A regex extracts the field-of-invention sentence. Pass 2 then uses that extracted sentence as its query rather than a generic domain string. Both passes are combined and deduplicated.
tasks/task_factory.py — build_scrutiny_task() now has a Mandatory Step 0: the agent must quote the field-of-invention and novelty sentences verbatim from the document before any analysis. If it cannot find them, it must say "NOT FOUND IN DOCUMENT" and stop — it cannot invent them. A new explicit host-device scope guard with a heater/LCD example is embedded in the prompt.
ui/tab_scrutiny.py — After scrutiny runs, a green verification banner shows the user what field of invention was detected: "🔬 Detected field of invention: a flexible polymer resistive heater film bonded to an LCD." If they see the wrong field displayed, they know immediately to fix their source document's abstract section before trusting the questions.
ui/session_state.py — Added field_of_invention key to session state defaults.

Recommendation for your Draft1 documents
The most reliable fix is also structural: make sure every patent info sheet starts with a clear, distinct "Field of the Invention" heading followed by one sentence naming the actual invention. The regex extractor specifically searches for this heading. The heater patent should have: "The present invention relates to a flexible polymer resistive heater film configured for bonding to the surface of an LCD display panel." That one sentence would have prevented the entire failure.
